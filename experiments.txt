# Energy Consumption Forecasting Project - Experiment Log

## Experiment 1: Baseline Model

### Dataset

* Appliances Energy Prediction Dataset
* Records: 19,735
* Target Variable: Appliances

### Features Used

* Appliances
* T_out
* RH_out
* Windspeed
* T1
* RH_1
* T2
* RH_2
* hour
* day_of_week
* month
* is_weekend

### Sequence Length

* 24 timesteps

### Model Architecture

* LSTM Layers: 2
* Hidden Size: 64
* Dropout: 0.2
* Dense Layer: 32 neurons
* Optimizer: Adam
* Loss Function: MSE
* Epochs: 20

### Results

* MAE: 0.0248
* RMSE: 0.0550
* R² Score: 0.5808

### Observations

* Stable convergence during training.
* No significant overfitting observed.
* Validation loss remained close to training loss.
* Hour of day emerged as the most important predictor.

---

## Feature Importance Analysis

### Most Important Features

1. hour (~0.177)
2. T_out (~0.133)
3. RH_1 (~0.124)
4. RH_2 (~0.122)
5. RH_out (~0.108)

### Least Important Features

1. is_weekend (~0.004)
2. month (~0.020)
3. day_of_week (~0.042)

### Observation

* Time-of-day is the strongest predictor of household energy consumption.
* Weekend and month contribute very little predictive power.
* Future experiments should evaluate removing weak features.

## Experiment 2: Reduced Feature Set

Removed Features:
- month
- is_weekend

Sequence Length:
24

Results:
MAE  : 0.0315
RMSE : 0.0572
R²   : 0.5460

Comparison with Baseline:
- MAE increased
- RMSE increased
- R² decreased


Conclusion:
Removing low-importance features degraded model performance.
The original 12-feature model was retained.
